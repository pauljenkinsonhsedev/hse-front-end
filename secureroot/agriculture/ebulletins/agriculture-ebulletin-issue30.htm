HSE Agriculture e-Bulletin January 2014

May we start by wishing a Happy and Safe New Year to all our subscribers. In this packed edition of the e:bulletin, we welcome our new Head of Agriculture in HSE and have a reminder about child safety around the farm. There are three reports looking into agricultural incidents and a safety alert, along with a seasonal message for pregnant women. We also take a look at some of the bogus ‘Elf and Safety’ excuses, in our Myth map.

As always Come Home Safe.

HSE has appointed a new Head of Agriculture

Rick Brunt moves from his position as head of field operations for the North West to replace Graeme Walker who retired from HSE after 37 years service.

As the head of agriculture, Rick will work closely with the industry and will lead a Unit of 11 specialist inspectors, policy and support staff.   

Rick said:

“It has been nearly nine years since I was last working closely with the industry, and while much has changed, many of the underlying problems are all too familiar.”

“Under Graeme’s leadership a lot has been achieved in recent years, and I look forward to building on those achievements and once again working with and supporting the agricultural industry as it promotes a culture where risk isn’t accepted as a given and those involved in the industry recognise that the toll of deaths and injuries can be further reduced.”

Child Safety

HSE is currently investigating a number of fatal incidents involving children on farms.  Whilst every death is a tragedy, those involving children are particularly harrowing.  HSE has a number of resources dedicated to child safety on the farm please take time to read them and avoid your children becoming statistic.

Preventing Accidents to Children

A Warning to Pregnant Women

As the lambing and calving season gets underway, pregnant women should avoid close contact with farm animals that are giving birth, to avoid the risk to their own health and that of their unborn child.

DEFRA Guidance

Safety Alert

HSE has become aware of a dangerous practice involving the fitting of non-standard accessories, to brush cutting machines. In particular, the supply of chain flail attachments comprising a cutting head incorporating lengths of metal chain. This alert is relevant to any metal brush cutter accessory manufactured or assembled from more than one component.

New and Updated Guidance

Information on controlling substances that are hazardous to the health of workers has been updated to help businesses comply with legal requirements.

More information - COSHH

HSE has published new help for employers on how to protect the health, safety and welfare of their workers.

More Information - Health and safety of workers

Myth Map of Britain

The Myth Busters Challenge Panel was set up in April 2012 to help the public challenge petty and misguided decisions wrongly made in the name of health and safety. Since then there has been more than 210 complaints to the Panel, with the huge majority of cases having no basis in law. HSE has produced a UK map of bogus ‘elf and safety' excuses.

Reports

Fatal injuries in farming, forestry, horticulture and associated industries 2012/13

Figures published in the Health and Safety Executive’s report 'Fatal injuries in farming, forestry, horticulture and associated industries 2012/13' show that 38 people were killed as a result of farming and other agriculture-related activities during the year.

The report shows that transport-related injuries were responsible for more deaths than any other cause. It also highlights trends and indicates key points in fatalities involving employees, the self-employed and members of the public.

Fatals report

Pesticides incidents reports 2012/13

This report provides information on incidents and complaints involving pesticides investigated by the Health and Safety Executive.

It includes a summary of the alleged ill health incidents considered by the Pesticide Incidents Appraisal Panel (PIAP) including an analysis of the environmental and other non-health complaints investigated during the year.

Pesticide incidents report

Figures published HSE show an 11 per cent drop in major injuries compared to 2011/12

The provisional statistics published by HSE show that in Britain between April 2012 and March 2013 there has been little change in the industries in which workers are most likely to be injured by their jobs – with construction, agriculture and, waste and recycling among the higher risk sectors.

Incidents

A Dumfries estate has been fined for health and safety failings after a worker died during tree felling operations.  The worker died after being struck by a 36 metre tall tree, which had been knocked over by another tree being felled.

Preventing Falls Guidance

The Buccleuch Estates Limited has been fined for health and safety failings after a worker died during tree felling operations at Bogrie Wood near Drumlanrig Castle in Dumfries.

Treework guidance
